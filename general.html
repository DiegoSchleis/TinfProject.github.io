General

Formula One (also Formula 1 or F1 and officially the FIA Formula One World Championship) is the highest class of single-seat auto racing that is sanctioned by the Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile (FIA). The FIA Formula One World Championship has been the premier form of racing since the inaugural season in 1950, although other Formula One races were regularly held until 1983. The "formula", designated in the name, refers to a set of rules, to which all participants' cars must conform. The F1 season consists of a series of races, known as Grands Prix (from French, meaning grand prizes), held worldwide on purpose-built F1 circuits and public roads. Drivers of this class of motor sport racing have to firstly win in other lower classes, for example Formula 3 or Formula E etc. After that they are recruited by the F1 teams such as Williams Martini Racing to drive in simulators at their factory or even substitute other F1 drivers that are currently driving in the season and are injured or similar.
